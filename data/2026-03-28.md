### [1] [Large-scale weak lensing convergence in nonlinear general relativity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24838)
*Hayley J. Macpherson*

Main category: astro-ph.CO

TL;DR: 该研究使用端到端非线性广义相对论框架研究弱引力透镜收敛性，通过数值相对论模拟和广义相对论光线追踪，在20个合成观测者上比较非线性计算与微扰理论预测，发现在ℓ<100的大角度尺度上，线性微扰理论预测与非线性结果偏差在3-30%之间。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究动机是使用完全非线性广义相对论框架来研究弱引力透镜收敛性，特别是在大角度尺度(ℓ<100)上，检验线性微扰理论在描述非线性引力效应方面的准确性。

Method: 方法结合了数值相对论模拟大尺度结构形成和广义相对论光线追踪技术，创建了20个合成观测者的数据集，在红移范围0.05<z<3内分析弱引力透镜收敛性。

Result: 结果发现：1) 在红移z<0.6时，多普勒透镜效应很重要；2) 线性微扰理论平均预测非线性收敛性在3-30%误差范围内；3) 较小角度尺度比较大角度尺度与线性理论匹配更好；4) 差异大多低于宇宙方差水平。

Conclusion: 结论是线性微扰理论在描述弱引力透镜收敛性方面表现良好，特别是在小角度尺度上，虽然存在系统差异但大多在宇宙方差范围内，验证了微扰理论在宇宙学研究中的适用性。

Abstract: In this work we investigate the weak lensing convergence using an end-to-end nonlinear general relativistic framework. Combining numerical relativity simulations of large-scale structure formation with general relativistic ray-tracing, we compare our nonlinear calculation to the expectation based on perturbation theory for a set of 20 synthetic observers. We focus on large angular scales $\ell < 100$ across a broad range of redshifts with $0.05<z<3$. We confirm the importance of Doppler lensing for redshifts below $z\sim$0.6, as predicted by previous works. On average across our observers, linear perturbation theory predicts the nonlinear convergence to within 3-30% across all redshifts and angular scales we study. In general, we find smaller angular scales are better matched by linear theory than larger angular scales. While we cannot definitively identify the source of the discrepancy, for our particular study of redshift slices on observers' light cones the differences are mostly below the level of cosmic variance.

Main category: astro-ph.CO

TL;DR: 该研究使用频率论轮廓似然法约束暗物质-质子散射模型，避免了贝叶斯方法中的先验体积效应，发现贝叶斯方法会高估散射截面的约束上限。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究动机是解决暗物质-质子散射模型约束中的先验体积效应问题。当散射截面或相互作用暗物质比例趋近于零时，贝叶斯方法中的后验分布会偏向该参数空间区域，导致约束估计偏差。

Method: 采用频率论轮廓似然技术进行先验独立的约束分析，并与贝叶斯方法对比。使用Planck 2018宇宙微波背景各向异性数据，分析速度无关的暗物质-质子散射模型，包括只有部分暗物质具有非引力相互作用的情况。

Conclusion: 对于存在先验体积效应的模型（如暗物质散射模型），建议在贝叶斯约束基础上补充频率论统计分析，以更好地评估先验分布的影响，避免产生有偏差的上限约束。

Abstract: We present profile-likelihood constraints on velocity-independent dark matter-proton scattering, including cases in which only a fraction of dark matter has such non-gravitational interactions. Frequentist profile-likelihood techniques provide prior-independent constraints, circumventing prior-volume effects that we show arise in Bayesian constraints on this model. In the limit where the scattering cross section or the fraction of interacting dark matter approaches zero, the other interacting dark matter model parameters become unconstrained, causing the posterior distribution to favor that region of parameter space. Using Planck 2018 cosmic microwave background anisotropy data, we find a clear impact of prior-volume effects on the posteriors used to place constraints on dark matter scattering. Compared to the frequentist analysis, the Bayesian method consistently overestimates the constraints on the cross section. Given the potentially biased upper limits on models subject to prior-volume effects, such as this one, we recommend supplementing Bayesian constraints with frequentist statistics to better assess the impact of priors.

Main category: astro-ph.GA

TL;DR: 该研究通过数值模拟发现，模糊暗物质（FDM）的波动特性会延迟早期宇宙恒星形成，这种延迟与晕质量和轴子质量成反比，并导致恒星形成从中心单块模式转向低质量碎片化星团。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究模糊暗物质（由超轻轴子组成）的波动特性如何影响早期宇宙恒星形成，特别是其小尺度动力学如何影响冷密气体的积累，这对于完善轴子质量的观测约束至关重要。

Method: 使用arepo代码和axirepo模块，模拟了不同轴子质量（1×10⁻²² eV至7×10⁻²² eV）和晕质量（3×10⁸ M⊙至8×10⁹ M⊙）范围内的晕形成过程，研究了FDM密度场的小尺度动力学对冷密气体积累的影响。

Conclusion: FDM的波动特性导致恒星形成从冷暗物质中的单块中心模式转向低质量碎片化星团，这些内部低效机制提供了延迟宇宙黎明超越功率谱截断效应的物理机制，对完善轴子质量的观测约束至关重要。

Abstract: Fuzzy Dark Matter (FDM), composed of ultra-light axions ($m_a \sim 1 \times 10^{-22}$ eV), exhibits wave-like properties that can significantly impact early-universe star formation. Using the arepo code with the axirepo module, we simulate the assembly of haloes across a range of axion masses ($1 \times 10^{-22}$ eV $\le m_a \le 7 \times 10^{-22} $ eV) and halo masses ($3 \times 10^{8} M_\odot \le M_h \le 8 \times 10^{9} M_\odot$). We find that the delay in star formation scales inversely with both halo mass and axion mass. While the static, cored geometry of the soliton primarily sets the timing of the initial collapse, we identify a secondary dynamical barrier driven by stochastic fluctuations that is most potent at the low-mass end of our parameter space. These dynamics dictate the spatial scale of dense gas by injecting kinetic energy and inducing significant angular momentum, which can rotationally stabilize gas out to the soliton radius. This wave-driven stirring leads to the formation of extended $\text{H}_2$ plumes and promotes dynamical mixing, effectively starving the central regions and forcing gas to cool in a more fragmented, diffuse manner. Our results indicate a shift from the monolithic central star formation seen in CDM toward lower-mass, fragmented clusters. These internal inefficiencies provide a physical mechanism for delaying Cosmic Dawn beyond the effects of the power spectrum cut-off, which is essential for refining observational constraints on the axion mass.
